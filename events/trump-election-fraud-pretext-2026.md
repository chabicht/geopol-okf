# Background

[Trump]({{ site.baseurl }}/actors/trump.html)'s second term has been characterized by escalating rhetorical assaults on democratic norms — the [Slaughter SCOTUS ruling]({{ site.baseurl }}/events/slaughter-scotus-ruling.html), anticommunism as a political weapon, and systematic institutional defacement. The [US-Iran War]({{ site.baseurl }}/conflicts/us-iran-war-2026.html) was supposed to deliver a victory narrative for the midterm elections: the economy would recover, the Strait would reopen, and voters would put the gas price shock behind them.

# Current Situation

With the [Hormuz ceasefire collapse]({{ site.baseurl }}/events/hormuz-ceasefire-collapse-2026.html), Trump's electoral strategy has shifted dramatically. Having given up on extracting anything resembling victory from the Iran war, Trump announced a primetime speech reportedly about 2020 election fraud, with some reports hinting he may try to declare the two Democratic senators from Georgia illegitimate [1].

[Paul Krugman]({{ site.baseurl }}/actors/authors/paul-krugman.html) argues this is not a genuine legal challenge but the stage-setting for massive interference in the November midterms. The claim that Trump won the 2020 election will convince no one, but the purpose is not persuasion — it is laying the pretext for blocking fair elections, possibly blocking elections entirely [1].

# Analysis

## The Connection Between Foreign Policy Failure and Domestic Repression

Krugman identifies the causal chain: Trump's inability to admit the Iran war failed eliminated the "economy is great, we won" electoral strategy. Without that narrative, the administration has decided that some combination of propaganda, misinformation, disinformation, and massive illegality is their path forward. The foreign policy failure is not causing the authoritarian turn — the authoritarian impulse was always present — but it has removed the last constraint of electoral pragmatism. When winning at the ballot box is no longer feasible, the groundwork for preventing the vote is laid [1].

## The Pattern of "They Wouldn't Do That"

Krugman warns that the proposition that there are things even Trump would not do has been "the best way to be wrong about everything, every step of the Trump administration." The escalation from foreign policy failure to domestic electoral interference follows a pattern visible throughout the term: each time observers assume a floor of restraint, the administration breaks through it [1].

See [Ideology and Democracy]({{ site.baseurl }}/themes/ideology-and-democracy.html) for the broader pattern of democratic backsliding.
