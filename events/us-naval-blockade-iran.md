# Background

After the initial kinetic phase of the [US-Iran War of 2026]({{ site.baseurl }}/conflicts/us-iran-war-2026.html), the United States imposed a naval blockade on Iranian ports — a counter-blockade designed to pressure Iran by cutting off its oil exports. This created a dual-blockade architecture: Iran's closure of Hormuz blocked inbound traffic, while the US outer-ring blockade blocked Iranian exports. The strategy drew historical parallels to Napoleonic blockade warfare, where blockades generate adaptive responses that ultimately undermine their effectiveness [1].

# What Happened

- The US imposed a naval blockade on Iranian ports, creating an outer-ring interception capability alongside the existing Hormuz-focused operations [2].
- US Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent implemented secondary sanctions on Chinese banks facilitating Iranian oil trade, extending the blockade into the financial system [2].
- Iran threatened to block the Red Sea in response, expanding the conflict's geographic scope [2].
- The US struck the South Pars gas field — Iran's largest — in an escalation that also affected [Qatar]({{ site.baseurl }}/actors/countries/qatar.html), which shares the field [2].
- The USS George H.W. Bush carrier group was deployed to enforce the blockade [2].
- The Pentagon sought help from automakers to produce military vehicles, signaling industrial capacity constraints [2].

# Analysis

[The Honest Sorcerer]({{ site.baseurl }}/actors/authors/the-honest-sorcerer.html) argues the counter-blockade revealed the war's true objective: controlling oil flow to [China]({{ site.baseurl }}/actors/countries/china.html), not uranium enrichment. By blockading Iranian ports while imposing secondary sanctions on Chinese banks, the US was attempting to sever the energy pipeline sustaining the DragonBear axis [2].

[Endtropy]({{ site.baseurl }}/actors/authors/endtropy.html) draws the historical parallel to Napoleon's Continental System: a blockade designed to deny the adversary resources instead generates adaptive trade networks, smuggling, and financial workarounds that ultimately weaken the blockading power. The US counter-blockade accelerated the dedollarization of Chinese-Iranian trade, as the secondary sanctions on Chinese banks pushed trade into non-dollar channels [1].
