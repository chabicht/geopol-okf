# Background

The [June 17 Versailles Memorandum]({{ site.baseurl }}/events/iran-ceasefire-mou.html) ended the kinetic phase of the [US-Iran War of 2026]({{ site.baseurl }}/conflicts/us-iran-war-2026.html), producing a fragile ceasefire that left [Iran]({{ site.baseurl }}/actors/countries/iran.html)'s coercive infrastructure intact.

# Current Situation

On July 7-8, 2026, the ceasefire collapsed: commercial vessels were attacked in the Strait of Hormuz, the US Treasury revoked the oil waiver, US forces struck Iranian military bases, Iran claimed retaliatory strikes against 85 US installations, and Trump declared the Memorandum "over" at the NATO summit. Around July 13, Trump reinstated the blockade and Iran resumed drone strikes. See [US-Iran War]({{ site.baseurl }}/conflicts/us-iran-war-2026.html) for the full tactical timeline, escalation trap analysis, and market repricing details.

# Analysis

Krugman argues the collapse will do less economic damage than expected because the binding constraint has shifted from crude supply to refining capacity (10% of world refining offline, primarily from Ukraine's drone campaign against Russian refineries). See [US-Iran War]({{ site.baseurl }}/conflicts/us-iran-war-2026.html) — "Ceasefire Collapse" and "The Repricing of Rupture" sections for full analysis [1][2].
