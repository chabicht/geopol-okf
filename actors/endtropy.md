# Background

Endtropy (writing as "John Doe" on the Substack publication "endtropy") is a pseudonymous analyst whose "Operation Epic Folly(Fury)" series provides a historical-analogical framework for understanding the US-Iran War. His approach draws on Napoleonic history — the Continental System, the Battle of Trafalgar, and the counter-blockade dynamics of the early 19th century — to illuminate the structural patterns of economic warfare, chokepoint control, and the cascading consequences of disrupting global trade flows.

# Key Frameworks

## Blockade Warfare as Historical Pattern

Endtropy draws direct parallels between Napoleon's Continental System (the attempt to blockade British trade) and the US-Iran counter-blockade dynamics. The historical lesson: blockades appear decisive in theory but generate adaptive responses that undermine their effectiveness. The counter-blockade — Iran's closure of Hormuz in response to the US naval blockade of Iranian ports — mirrors the British response to Napoleon: the blockaded power uses naval advantage to redirect trade and impose costs on the blockading power [1].

## Petrodollar as Geopolitical Infrastructure

The analysis of the [UAE](/actors/uae.md)'s swap-line request frames the petrodollar not as a financial abstraction but as geopolitical infrastructure — the plumbing that connects US military power to global energy markets. When that plumbing shows stress (swap-line requests, gold accumulation, currency diversification), it signals that the geopolitical order the plumbing supports is also under stress. The monetary dimension of the Iran war is not a side effect but a central theater [2].
