# Background

The Social Design Agency (SDA) is a Russian PR firm already sanctioned by the US, UK, and [EU]({{ site.baseurl }}/actors/organizations/eu.html) for influence campaigns. Internal documents reveal that Russian Presidential Administration officials oversee the SDA's work, making it an instrument of state-directed [cognitive warfare]({{ site.baseurl }}/themes/cognitive-warfare.html) rather than an independent operation. The SDA represents the formalization of Russian influence operations — a PR firm repurposed as a weapon of strategic destabilization [1].

# Current Situation

## Doctrine: "Cognitive Strikes" and the Superpower-Image Objective

Internal documents describe the SDA's operations as "cognitive strikes" against the West — a terminology that frames influence operations not as propaganda or disinformation but as offensive cognitive warfare, targeted attacks on the psychological and social infrastructure of Western societies [1]. The stated goal is to help [Russia]({{ site.baseurl }}/actors/countries/russia.html) "maintain the image of a superpower." The internal logic is explicit: "The more Russia participates in active influence campaigns all over the world, the stronger the image of a global Russian power." This reveals that Russian cognitive warfare serves a dual purpose — destabilizing adversaries while compensating for the gap between Russia's actual capabilities and its superpower aspirations [1]. The doctrinal framing thus links tactical operations to a strategic objective: the SDA does not merely disrupt Western societies, it manufactures the appearance of Russian global reach as a substitute for the conventional power projection that Russia can no longer sustain.

## Operational Portfolio and Capability

The SDA's operations span vandalism attacks in France and Germany — physical provocations designed to amplify social tensions — pro-Russian messaging cultivated through Western opinion leaders, and direct election interference [1]. These are not isolated tactics but components of an integrated cognitive warfare campaign. The operational scope extends beyond the West into the post-Soviet space: the SDA planned to influence Armenia's parliamentary election through a media outlet aimed at Russian-speaking voters, targeting [Russia]({{ site.baseurl }}/actors/countries/russia.html)'s near abroad through media infrastructure rather than covert sabotage [1]. The SDA also orchestrated islamophobic pig head attacks on Paris mosques — an operation that became public through the [Paris pig head attack event]({{ site.baseurl }}/events/paris-pig-head-attack.html), exemplifying the agency's approach of low-cost physical provocations designed to trigger maximum social amplification through media coverage and political reaction [1].

# Analysis

The Social Design Agency represents the professionalization of Russian cognitive warfare — a PR firm operating under Presidential Administration oversight with explicit strategic objectives, documented operational planning, and a global reach. The "cognitive strikes" framing reveals a doctrinal shift: Russia treats the psychological and social fabric of Western societies as a battlefield, not merely a target for propaganda. The SDA's dual function — projecting superpower image while destabilizing adversaries — makes it a structural feature of Russian grand strategy, not an aberration. The sanctions regime has not halted operations; it has merely increased the cost of attribution [1].
