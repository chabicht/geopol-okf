# Background

Heather Cox Richardson is a political historian and author of a daily Substack newsletter that provides running commentary on the [Trump]({{ site.baseurl }}/actors/trump.html) administration, American democratic institutions, and the historical context of contemporary political events. Her approach combines day-to-day political tracking with longer historical arcs, drawing on her expertise in American political history to contextualize the erosion of democratic norms.

# Key Frameworks

## Corruption as Governance

Richardson systematically tracks the monetization of presidential power, documenting specific revenue streams and conflicts of interest. Her July 5, 2026 dispatch records Trump's $1.4 billion in cryptocurrency venture profits since taking office, the near-total collapse of his memecoin (from $75.35 to $1.76, a 97% drop that cost nearly a million followers $3.81 billion in collective losses while Trump took transaction fees up front), and the Freedom 250 scheme that diverted donations from the bipartisan America250 program to Trump's own organization [1].

## Anticommunism as Political Weapon

Richardson identifies Trump's July 2026 pivot to anticommunist rhetoric as a sign of panic rather than confidence. Trump's Mt. Rushmore speech framing political opponents as "communists" — "the greatest threat to our country, including World War I, World War II, Pearl Harbor, or even 9/11" — is, in Richardson's analysis, a fabricated narrative constructed after the electoral success of Democratic Socialists in primary elections and the popularity of New York City Mayor Zohran Mamdani. She notes the irony that it is Trump, not his opponents, who is taking government stakes in private enterprises — the actual definition of communism [1].

## Democratic Socialists vs Communists

Richardson draws a careful distinction: Democratic Socialists embrace private enterprise alongside government provision of services like healthcare, education, and infrastructure — positions that echo Republican platforms from the 1860s (public colleges) through the 1900s (Theodore Roosevelt's public health insurance) and the 1950s (Eisenhower's 91% top marginal tax rate). The conflation of Democratic Socialism with communism is, in her analysis, a deliberate political strategy rather than an analytical error [1].

## Institutional Defacement

Richardson documents the physical and symbolic degradation of American institutions: the Lincoln Memorial Reflecting Pool turning green with algae and its coating breaking loose, the poorly attended Great American State Fair with its cheap-looking triumphal arch, the deletion of aerial images showing empty National Mall space from official social media accounts. These details complement [Anne Applebaum]({{ site.baseurl }}/actors/authors/anne-applebaum.html)'s institutional defacement framework with ground-level observation [1].

## OBBBA vs the American Rescue Plan

Richardson's July 15, 2026 dispatch frames the contrast between the Democrats' 2021 American Rescue Plan — whose expanded child tax credit lifted 3.7 million children out of poverty before it expired — and the Republicans' 2025 One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA) as the central statement of what the two parties believe government is for. The OBBBA extended over $4.5 trillion in tax breaks for the wealthy and corporations while cutting more than $1 trillion from social welfare programs; between its passage in July 2025 and March 2026, more than 4 million people lost access to the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program. The same dispatch documents the $95 billion Republican budget framework — maneuvered through reconciliation to avoid Democratic votes — that would fund the unpopular [Iran war]({{ site.baseurl }}/conflicts/us-iran-war-2026.html), a farmer bailout for Trump's tariff wars, and aspects of the SAVE America voter-suppression measure that Trump could not pass through normal legislative order [2].

## Authoritarian Symbolism: The Coin, the Helipad, the Courts

Richardson's July 16, 2026 dispatch tracks the consolidation of authoritarian symbolism through mid-2026: the Treasury's commemorative $1 coin with [Trump]({{ site.baseurl }}/actors/trump.html)'s likeness (a practice associated with living political leaders who try to make themselves indistinguishable from the government — Assad, Idi Amin, Saddam Hussein); the helipad Trump is building on the South Lawn without congressional or review-panel permission, funded by Lockheed Martin; the Reflecting Pool renovation failing at the seams with the Department of Justice arresting a 67-year-old cyclist for "vandalism"; Acting Attorney General Todd Blanche — Trump's former criminal defense attorney — slipping during his confirmation hearing; the federal judges in Seattle whose unanimously appointed U.S. attorney was fired by email within an hour of taking the oath. A Washington Post/Ipsos poll showed Trump's approval at 37%, with only 15% of Americans "strongly" approving — the lowest strong-approval figure on record [3].

## The *Unhumans* Ideology and Dehumanization as Governance

Richardson's July 17, 2026 dispatch documents the Trump administration's embrace of the far-right conspiracy theory articulated in Jack Posobiec and Joshua Lisec's *Unhumans: The Secret History of Communist Revolutions (and How to Crush Them)* — blurbed by Donald Trump Jr. and J.D. Vance and introduced by Stephen Bannon. The book's argument that anyone from communists to "progressives" places themselves "outside the category" of humanity and must be "unhumaned" first provided the framing for Stephen Miller's State Department speech and Bannon's *War Room* podcast argument that the communist-threat narrative is "a predicate for immediate action." Richardson traces the same day's DHS Secretary Mullin threatening state officials with criminal prosecution for refusing to purge voter rolls through a federal tool that federal judges have blocked sixteen times. The full analysis is dissolved into [United States]({{ site.baseurl }}/actors/countries/united-states.html), [Ideology and Democracy]({{ site.baseurl }}/themes/ideology-and-democracy.html), and [Trump Election Interference Pretext]({{ site.baseurl }}/events/trump-election-fraud-pretext-2026.html) [4].
