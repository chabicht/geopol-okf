# Background

Geopolitical Futures is a strategic analysis publication founded by George Friedman that provides structured assessments of global geopolitical dynamics. GPF's approach emphasizes structural drivers — geography, demographics, military capacity, and economic fundamentals — over personality-driven analysis. The publication produces detailed country and regional assessments, often accompanied by maps that illustrate the geographic constraints driving political outcomes.

# Key Frameworks

## Structural Drivers Over Personality

GPF's analytical approach prioritizes structural factors — geographic constraints, demographic trajectories, institutional capacity — over the individual leader decisions that [O'Brien](/actors/authors/phillips-obrien.md) emphasizes. This creates a complementary tension: GPF explains why structural pressures exist, while O'Brien explains why the timing and direction of action depend on the specific leader in power.

## Case Studies in the Bundle

GPF analysis appears in the bundle covering:
- **2026 Forecast — Re-anchoring the World**: US-China accommodation as the most important geopolitical issue; Russia as regional power; EU devolving into multiregional system; Middle East, South Asia, Africa, and Latin America regional forecasts [7]
- **China's CMC purge**: The structural rupture in China's military command, driven by Xi's insistence on a 2027 Taiwan timeline versus Zhang Youxia's professional caution about a 2035 timeline [1]
- **China's Maoist ideological turn**: Cai Qi and Li Shulei's calls for ideological mobilization as a substitute for economic performance [8]
- **Horn of Africa competition**: Israel's Somaliland recognition, Turkey-Somalia military expansion, Red Sea port rivalry, and emerging bloc alignments [9]
- **Iran's internal politics**: Pre-war analysis of Iran's factional dynamics, IRGC erosion, and the rise of pragmatists ahead of the February 2026 revolution anniversary [2]
- **Layered security and alliance evolution**: Middle powers supplementing Cold War-era alliances with region-specific arrangements; CSTO as failed alliance model [10][11]
- **Turkey-Israel and US Middle East geostrategy**: US transition from Israel-centric to multilateral security; Turkey as dominant regional player [12]
- **Return of the nation-state in the Middle East**: US shift from fragmentation to preserving unified states; China's growing mediation role [13]
- **Southern flank erosion**: Russia's declining grip from the Black Sea through the South Caucasus to Central Asia; Trump Route corridor; Middle Corridor [14]
- **Tigray powder keg**: The Red Sea corridor competition and Ethiopian-Eritrean conflict dynamics [3]
- **Russia's Chechnya problem**: Kadyrov's succession crisis [4]
- **Mali and Turkish opportunity**: Russia's declining capacity in Africa [5]
- **Central Asia investment**: Chinese FDI patterns [6]
