World trade in goods and commercial services rose 7% in 2025 to US$34.65 trillion on a balance-of-payments basis, with goods up 6% and services up 8% (WTO). The 2025 picture, those are composite estimates of total 2025 exports of goods plus services for each of the three: EU ~$4.85tn (~14%), China ~$4.2tn (~12%), US ~$3.43tn (~9.9%), the same ordering as 2024, with all three roughly holding share. Two caveats worth noting. First, part of the EU's dollar-terms jump is currency effect: the euro appreciated substantially through 2025, so in euro terms extra-EU goods exports grew only 2%, versus China's 6.1% in dollars. The underlying volume story is one of China closing the gap, not the EU pulling away. Second, the composition diverged sharply by destination: EU exports to Switzerland rose 13.5% and to the US 3.6%, while exports to China fell 6.5%, whereas China's exports to the US fell 20% but grew 13.4% to ASEAN and 8.4% to the EU, very much in line with my Global System Bifurcation Thesis playing out in the trade data from the past year.
