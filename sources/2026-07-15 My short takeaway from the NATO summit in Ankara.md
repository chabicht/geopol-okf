NATO 3.0 is adjusting to the realities of new global competition. The conventional deterrence will be increasingly a responsibility for European NATO countries, while the US will stay engaged through the nuclear shield. Ukraine will build together with the Eastern Flank members the new conventional shield to prevent a direct kinetic war between Russia and Europe and will continue receiving military aid and tech transfer (eg Patriot license) while further integrating into the Euro-Atlantic architecture. NATO 3.0 will also be digitally integrated through a transatlantic warfighting cloud and alliance-wide AI adoption, and geographically stretched, treating the Black Sea-Mediterranean-Gulf chokepoints as one connected theater. NATO called out Iran on Hormuz navigation, and committed €27 billion to fuel logistics. NATO 3.0 isn’t just redistributing the European burden but it’s quietly expanding its geography southward, with Türkiye as the hinge.

Yet the summit’s unspoken lesson is that American commitment is now conditional and transactional, mostly priced in spending targets, trade ruptures, and territorial claims. This could be a strategic vulnerability the DragonBear will systematically probe below the Article 5 threshold through its hybrid warfare playbook of sabotage, cyber operations, election interference, and chokepoint pressure amid the “weaponization of everything”, testing whether a defence alliance held together by conditionality can still respond with urgent unity amid the new Cold War between America & China-Russia.
