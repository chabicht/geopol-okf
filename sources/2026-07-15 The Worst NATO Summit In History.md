Did you know that the NATO Leaders Summit in Ankara was a surprising success? Oh yes, indeed it was, and it has been reported widely. Using mostly anonymous sources, for instance, *[The](https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2026/07/08/denmark-says-it-will-defend-greenland-trump-threatens-revive-nato-crisis/)* *[Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2026/07/08/denmark-says-it-will-defend-greenland-trump-threatens-revive-nato-crisis/)* [reported that in closed session, President Trump supported the alliance strongly](https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2026/07/08/denmark-says-it-will-defend-greenland-trump-threatens-revive-nato-crisis/). And Trump himself, momentarily, played along with this charade. In his closing press conference on July 8, he said that the meeting showed the “tremendous unity” of NATO and furthermore claimed that the other NATO leaders were [“very smart people — they have a lot of good in their heart, not evil, good.”](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/watch-live-trump-holds-news-conference-at-nato-summit-in-turkey)

To add to the theatre of the absurd, Trump stated that there was “tremendous love in that room”.

*Note—a tell that Trump is lying is when he speaks about caring or human affection. He does not believe in such things towards other people and so, when he discusses them, it is a tell that he does not care at all. [Here is one of my favorite pieces on that phenomenon.](https://open.substack.com/pub/phillipspobrien/p/the-trump-tell?r=1tgexa&utm_campaign=post-expanded-share&utm_medium=web)*

And this is not all—the US supposedly made other major moves. The US nuclear umbrella over Europe was said to have been reconfirmed (when it was not), and Ukraine was promised help with its air defense (which so far has been meaningless).

However, this did not stop reporting of the summit from taking what Trump said, momentarily, at face value.

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!oCh3!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F7ade885b-17d2-4928-a1e0-87a5f8e13017_1156x1047.png)

And then the summit ended: Trump went back to bashing NATO, the defense commitment was revealed to be a poisoned chalice, and there has been no clarity at all about what help (if any) Ukraine was offered.

Maybe we will find out in two weeks.

Here are four ways in which the summit was not what it seemed and why it was the worst in history (which the participants actually admitted).

#### The US Threat To Greenland Stays

Let’s start with the actual trajectory of Trump’s rhetoric. The statement of NATO-love that he made on July 8 was sandwiched between a ramp-up of constant threats to take Greenland (in other words, to use force against a NATO partner). This started on [July 7, when Trump made an unveiled threat to take Greenland from Denmark](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/07/trump-nato-summit-greenland-us-troops-europe.html). He stated that Greenland “should be controlled by the United States, not by Denmark,” claimed Denmark doesn't spend money to help Greenland, repeated the nonsense claim that the island is surrounded by Chinese and Russian ships, and even threatened to remove all US forces from Europe if the Europeans did not bend to his will.

Then, on July 8, [Trump returned to his Greenland attacks, claiming that it was “stupid” that the USA gave the island “back” to Denmark](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-08/trump-s-greenland-obsession-returns-stupidly-we-gave-it-back) —strongly implying that he would seize it.

Then, on the plane ride back from Ankara, Trump went back on the offensive. He threatened to walk away from European defense if the USA did not get a “good deal” on Greenland. [When asked about the future of US forces in Europe, Trump replied](https://www.standard.co.uk/news/politics/donald-trump-greenland-keir-starmer-nato-deal-b1289313.html), “I haven’t made that final determination. A lot’s going to depend on Greenland.”

Can you feel the love?

So, in between one vague declaration of love, Trump made three explicit threats against a NATO country over three days before, during, and after the Ankara summit. This is by far the bigger story.

**The US Is Not Committed To Defending Europe—Far From It.**

This was not all. At the summit, the US expressly made no commitment to conventionally fight for Europe. [Here were the summit declaration’s words](https://www.nato.int/en/about-us/official-texts-and-resources/official-texts/2026/07/08/the-ankara-summit-declaration) on US commitment—note there was nothing specific about the USA at all, just a general statement that all states remain committed to the alliance.

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!N4yW!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd77ce7b5-8233-4faa-a112-9bdd8b630c80_1237x291.png)

This bland statement was hailed as a success, but simply masked the reality that the actual US policy now is to have a review, the purpose of which is to study drawing down US forces in Europe. Secretary Hegseth [stated this clearly on June 18](https://breakingdefense.com/2026/06/hegseth-announces-six-month-review-of-us-force-posture-in-europe/), when he announced the review and, btw, the report can take up to six months to be completed (if it stays on schedule). In other words, any promise made now is meaningless.

This review is a threat to Europe—basically do everything we want on basing, arms purchases, etc., or we will withdraw more of our forces. This is the reality. And Trump, in between his brief expression of love, sandwiched the Ankara summit with warnings to Europeans to bend to US will on basing and remaining strategically subservient.

Before the festivities, on July 2, he tweeted that “the United States spends more money on NATO than any other country, by far, to protect them, without getting any benefit from so doing.” At the summit kickoff, July 7, he even called Europe “a very different place”.

Moreover, at Ankara, Trump specifically called out major European states for harming the USA. [Here was his exact quote when asked about European support for his war with Iran.](https://www.france24.com/en/live-news/20260707-nato-allies-seek-to-win-over-trump-after-iran-ire)

*“I was testing to see whether or not they’d be there, because I’ve long said that we helped them…“Italy turned us down, and Germany turned us down, and France turned us down.”*

Finally, on the question of US commitment to NATO, the nuclear question was seen by some as a success. Yes, the USA might be winding down conventional forces in Europe, but they will still maintain the nuclear umbrella. This was something Mark Rutte stressed as the Ankara meeting was poised to start.

![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xnFXiJYlyhs)

I cannot imagine a more poorly conceived strategic position than one which has the US winding down conventional support for European NATO but still to be relied on for nuclear support. In other words, what we are hearing is that the US might not fight for the Baltic States (and it will not) but it will blow up the world if Putin uses a nuclear weapon on them.

This is simply not happening.

Look I could write a much longer piece on this, but it is part of the Trump administration still wanting Europe to be strategically reliant on the USA, while bullying them to do what he wants. A Europe without its own nuclear deterrent is not an independent Europe. That is what Trump wants.

Can you feel the love?

#### The Patriots For Ukraine Pledge

This might have been the most misunderstood or exaggerated moment of the whole summit. When meeting with President Zelensky, Trump made a very peculiar pledge to provide Ukraine with a license to make “Patriots” for themselves. [Here is his exact wording:](https://time.com/article/2026/07/08/trump-zelensky-ukraine-patriot-missile-interceptors-russia-nato/)

“We’re going to give a license to you to make Patriots. That’s pretty cool. This way, you can’t complain that we’re not giving ‘em enough…We’ll give them the right to make Patriots. We’ll show them how to do it.”

People instantly jumped to the conclusion that Ukraine would be making its own Patriots imminently or even that the US was promising more Patriot interceptors to Ukraine now. These are the moments when I wonder if I am going a little crazy. I tweeted this out after Trump spoke.

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Cjc2!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F046f84e1-4004-45a0-96a8-842f5f9ac825_952x1026.png)

I got lots of blowback for this, but I cannot see why. There is no clarity on this, we do not know whether this pledge is at all real, and all we do know is that for a long time this means little/no support for Ukraine as the Russians continue to hammer the country with ballistic missiles that the Ukrainians are struggling to shoot down. Here are the unanswered questions:

1. Will the license actually be granted? Since Trump’s statement there has been no update on this. So just hold your horses.
2. What will be covered by the license? Trump just spoke of “Patriots” and that means nothing. I made the mistake of assuming it means the most advanced interceptor missiles (PAC-3s)—but there is no guarantee of this at all. The Germans, for instance, only have a license for PAC-2s. For all we know, Ukraine might get a license to build interceptors that will not help with its most pressing problem.
3. Even with a license, it will probably take years to reach any reasonable rate of manufacture. This is an extremely complex process requiring many sub-contractors of advanced components (for which there are already long lists of purchasers). [If you want to get an idea of what will be needed to build Patriot interceptors, you could read this piece](https://www.fpri.org/article/2026/05/scaling-patriot-production-the-industrial-base-crisis-explained/). Thankfully, after the initial rush of extreme optimism about this move, this manufacturing problem began to be aired. *[The](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/07/09/world/europe/germany-japan-ukraine-patriot-missiles.html?smid=nytcore-ios-share)* *[New York Times](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/07/09/world/europe/germany-japan-ukraine-patriot-missiles.html?smid=nytcore-ios-share)* [produced a very useful story of the German and Japanese experiences](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/07/09/world/europe/germany-japan-ukraine-patriot-missiles.html?smid=nytcore-ios-share).

Also, Trump admitted he had never even discussed this with the US arms manufacturers who would have to be involved in this process. Indeed, there is no evidence that this was a real, worked-out pledge at all—and every sign that it was a spur-of-the-moment decision by Trump to get some plaudits.

I know people have great confidence in Ukrainian technical ingenuity and ability to scale up—but please hold your optimism in check on this. Ukraine cannot work magic and has actually struggled with many systems not nearly as advanced as the Patriot (see the FP-5 Flamingo, which has taken much longer than promised to be effective and appear in significant numbers).

The upshot of this move, if it means no existing Patriot interceptors from US stocks, is Putin being gifted years to hammer Ukraine with ballistic missiles and having limited capacity to shoot them down (unless Ukraine and the Europeans can come up with their own system).

**The Proof That This Was The Worst Summit In NATO History**

You want proof that this summit was the worst in history? Let us go back to the summit declaration and read the last paragraph.

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vfHP!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fa85543fb-a2a2-44c4-a9a7-f26efbd2e536_1186x136.png)

Our “next meeting”…interesting phrase that. Before Ankara, the next leadership summit was scheduled to be held in 2027, in Tirana, Albania. Yet, there is no mention of that meeting happening in this year’s statement. All we hear is some vague, “next” meeting happening sometime in the future at an indeterminate location.

And this is right—stories have started circulating that there will not be a leaders’ summit in 2027, [because European leaders see no reason to engage in these pointless/counterproductive meetings with Trump](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-08/nato-considers-skipping-2027-summit-to-avoid-tensions-with-trump).

This meeting was so bad there might not be another while Trump is President.

So, yes, this was the worst summit in NATO history, there were lies, deliberate papering over of cracks, strategic dreams, and benefits for Vladimir Putin.

But, hey, Trump felt the love, so that becomes the story.
